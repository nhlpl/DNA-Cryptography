**Pillar 2: Breakthrough Error Correction (Gungnir + THEA-code + Self-Synchronizing SDC)**

The **Gungnir** codec introduces a proof-of-work paradigm for DNA error correction. Gungnir also considers biochemical constraints including GC-content, homopolymers, and error-prone motifs during encoding.

**THEA-code** provides an autoencoder-based approach that efficiently generates IDS-correcting codes for complex insertion/deletion/substitution channels. Using disturbance-based discretization and a simulated differentiable IDS channel, THEA-code produces channel-customized IDS-correcting codes with commendable performance across realistic DNA storage channels, outperforming existing error-correcting codes in terms of decoding accuracy and storage capacity.

**Self-synchronizing single-deletion-correcting (SDC) codes** address the self-synchronization problem in DNA coding without using delimiters between codewords. Inspired by Francis Crick's "codes without commas" proposal for DNA sequences, these algorithms detect single-deletion errors while maintaining transmission efficiency, distinguishing the beginning and end of each codeword even in the presence of deletion errors.
